Fried Fish

Golden, crispy, and deliciously seasoned!

30 minutes Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 white fish fillets (about 150g each)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

1

Prepare the Batter

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, milk, water, baking powder, and salt until smooth. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Let it rest for 10 minutes while you prepare the fish.

2

Prepare the Fish

Pat the fish fillets dry with paper towels. This helps the batter adhere better. Cut larger fillets into smaller pieces if desired, about 10cm long.

3

Heat the Oil

Pour oil into a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to a depth of about 5cm. Heat to 365°F (185°C). Use a thermometer for accuracy, or test by dropping a small amount of batter in - it should sizzle and float immediately.

4

Coat and Fry

Dip each fish fillet into the batter, letting excess drip off. Carefully lower into the hot oil. Don't overcrowd - fry in batches if necessary. Fry for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy.

5

Drain and Serve

Remove fried fish with a slotted spoon and drain on a wire rack or paper towels. Sprinkle with a little extra salt while still hot. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and your favorite dipping sauce.

Tips & Variations

Gluten-Free Option

Use gluten-free flour blend instead of all-purpose flour.

Spicy Version

Add ½ tsp cayenne pepper or paprika to the batter.

Fish Alternatives

Works well with cod, haddock, tilapia, or catfish.

Serving Ideas

Serve in tacos, with fries, or as fish and chips.

Notes

  • For extra crispiness, you can double-dip the fish - coat in flour first, then batter.
  • Maintain oil temperature between 350-375°F for best results.
  • Leftovers can be reheated in a 375°F oven for 10 minutes to regain crispiness.
